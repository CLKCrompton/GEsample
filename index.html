About


The General e Grand Estoria is the only truly ecumenical work in the history of biblical exegesis and secular historiography. It is unique because of its respect for a variety of interpretations. We are faced with the first, and probably only, version of the Bible in which Christian, Jewish, Islamic, and apocryphal interpretations are included —a hermeneutic model superior to the later versions of Luther, King James, and Reina Valera. The GGE was revolutionary in the age of Latin literacy because it made available for readers of the vernacular a history of human experience, beginning with Adam and Eve and ending with the reign of the renowned patron of the arts and sciences, King Alfonso X of Castile (r. 1252-1284). It is of particular importance to literary history in that the compilers’ use of biblical commentaries to nuance the text of the Bible is one of the building blocks of medieval literary fiction.

This project aims to bring Digital Humanities tools to study the confluence of Jewish, Christian, and Muslim Biblical Interpretation in Medieval Spain and to offer the first English translation and critical edition of the first volume of the GGE to reflect a more comprehensive understanding of the text in its multicultural context. Our methodological process will consist of four major steps: i) a thorough reading of the relevant original manuscripts, ii) a paleographic-interpretative transcription, iii) a study of the multiple and heterogeneous collaborations within the Alfonsine text and iv) an establishment of a richly annotated critical edition.

Our team brings together experts in language, literature, religious studies, and digital humanities, working together to overcome the challenges that have prevented scholars working individually from producing a scholarly edition of this compendious work. The project aims to form a deep and comprehensive understanding of this unique and important text. In the first stage we produced a rigorously philological edition of the GGE. Our transcription of the text is the product of intensive study of its sources and reflects the text’s unique character as the most ecumenical work of historical and biblical exegesis. We also aim to locate and identify the different “hands and voices” that participated in this complex multicultural enterprise.

DESCRIPTION OF MANUSCRIPT

The main manuscript of the first part of the General e Grand Estoria is Mss 816, a codex called by the acronym A, which is kept in the National Library of Spain (BNE). The exact date of this copy is unknown, since it lacks a colophon, but it could be dated to 1275.

The text is a well-preserved parchment codex. It has 342 folios, arranged in 43 quaternions of 8 folios (except one of 6), with catchwords, and follows the Gregory’s rule, by which the flesh sides and the hair sides are respectively faced. It presents three cover pages at the beginning and at the end. The folios reach an approximate size of 410 x 292 mm. The foliation is modern, perhaps of the eighteenth century, and presents a couple of errors: after folio 25 the next one was ignored, which is now 25 bis; the numbering of folio 312 was omitted.

The text shows a very careful librarian gothic writing made by several hands, although it contains some errors and corrections. It presents a double column layout, with 41 lines each one. It has a discreet ornamentation in blue and red, colors also used for enlarged capital letters. The titles of the chapters appear in red.

Currently, the edition is an HTML-based critical edition, complete with notes and corresponding pictures of the manuscript. Forthcoming are many more aspects of the scholarly website such as a palaeographic edition of the manuscript and a critical edition of the text using the TEI, and a translation into English with notes from specialists in the field. Currently, the text is only available to GE team approved collaborators. If you would like a copy of the edition, please email us at EMAIL.


DESCRIPCIÓN MANUSCRITO

El manuscrito principal de la primera parte de la General e Grand Estoria es el Mss 816, un códice denominado por la sigla A, que se conserva en la Biblioteca Nacional de España. No se conoce la fecha exacta de su copia, puesto que carece de colofón, pero podría datarse hacia 1275.

Se trata de un códice en pergamino bien conservado. Tiene 342 folios, dispuestos en 43 cuadernos de 8 folios (menos uno de 6), con reclamos, y sigue la regla de Gregory, por la que se enfrentan los lados de carne y los lados de pelo, respectivamente. Presenta tres hojas de guarda al comienzo y al final, y los folios alcanzan un tamaño aproximado de 410 x 292 mm. La numeración es moderna, quizá del siglo XVIII, y presenta un par de errores: tras el folio 25 se pasó por alto el siguiente, que ahora es el 25 bis; se omitió la numeración del folio 312.

El texto muestra una escritura gótica libraria muy cuidada, aunque contiene algunos errores y correcciones, efectuada por varias manos. Está dispuesto a doble columna, de 41 líneas cada una. Presenta una discreta ornamentación en colores azul y rojo, colores usados también para las mayúsculas agrandadas. En rojo figuran los títulos de los capítulos.
